## 1. Executive Summary & Product Vision

### 1.1 The Ultimate Objective
This project is an interactive, handcrafted digital birthday gift. It exists to bridge the physical gap between Singapore and Jombang. The software must not feel like software; it must feel like a tactile, emotional artifact. The primary metric for success is not time-on-page or conversion, but the precise evocation of warmth, comfort, and feeling genuinely loved.

### 1.2 The Narrative Paradigm: The Digital Unboxing
Users do not "scroll" through this website; they "unwrap" it. The experience is designed as a linear emotional progression that visually manifests as opening a physical gift, reading a letter, walking through a living botanical garden, discovering memories, and culminating in the assembly of a bespoke floral bouquet. The interface is completely subordinate to the narrative.

### 1.3 The Distance Philosophy
Distance in this project is never framed as a deficit or a source of sadness. The spatial gap between Singapore and Jombang is visually translated into a glowing, warm thread—proof of the effort and reach of affection. The technology serves to compress this geography into a single, intimate viewport.

## 2. Global Design Language & Visual System

### 2.1 Color Architecture
The color palette is meticulously calibrated to replicate the soft, warm tones of a late afternoon botanical garden (Golden Hour) mixed with premium stationery.

* **Ivory Paper (Base Background):** `#FDFBF7`. This is the foundational canvas. It must always be paired with the SVG noise texture overlay to prevent it from looking like a flat digital white.
* **Warm Blush (Primary Accent):** `#E8B4B8`. Used for interactive states, soft glows, and the core petals of the primary rose.
* **Dusty Rose (Secondary Accent):** `#C98A92`. Used for typography highlights, wax seal coloring, and active states.
* **Sage Green (Foliage/Structural):** `#A5B49F`. Used for stems, leaves, UI borders, and inactive progress states.
* **Champagne Gold (Illumination):** `#D4AF37`. Used exclusively for lighting effects, fireflies, the distance thread, and magical transitions.
* **Deep Espresso (Primary Text):** `#2C2621`. Never use pure black (`#000000`). Deep Espresso provides high legibility while maintaining the warmth of ink on paper.

### 2.2 Typography System
Typography must evoke the elegance of a wedding invitation and the intimacy of a handwritten journal.

* **Primary Display (Headings & Large Quotes):** *Playfair Display*.
    * Weight: 400 (Regular) and 500 (Medium) only.
    * Usage: Chapter titles, prominent emotional statements.
    * Tracking: `-0.02em` to create a tight, editorial feel.
* **Body & UI (Readability):** *Lato*.
    * Weight: 300 (Light) and 400 (Regular).
    * Usage: Paragraphs, UI labels, descriptions.
    * Line-Height: `1.8` for breathability.
* **Handwritten Accent (Personal Notes):** *Dancing Script*.
    * Weight: 400.
    * Usage: Signatures, the birthday letter, small annotations on polaroids.
    * Transformation: Apply a slight random rotation (`-1deg` to `1deg`) in CSS to prevent machine-like perfection.

### 2.3 The Paper & Tactility Standard
The screen must feel textured.

* **Global Noise Overlay:** A fixed, `pointer-events: none` div covering the viewport (`z-index: 9999`). It uses a base64 encoded SVG of fine monochromatic noise, set to `mix-blend-mode: multiply` and `opacity: 0.04`.
* **Shadows:** Never use default drop-shadows. Shadows must simulate physical paper lifting off a desk. Use multi-layered shadows: `box-shadow: 0 4px 12px rgba(44, 38, 33, 0.05), 0 1px 3px rgba(44, 38, 33, 0.08);`.
* **Glassmorphism:** Used strictly for the Memory Garden polaroids and meaning cards. `backdrop-filter: blur(12px) saturate(120%)` with a `rgba(253, 251, 247, 0.4)` background and a `1px solid rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.6)` border to catch the light.

## 3. The Living World System (Environment Physics)
The background is a living, breathing entity. It operates on a continuous, uninterrupted loop regardless of user interaction, creating a sense of enduring life.

### 3.1 Dynamic Sunlight & Illumination
* **Visual Behavior:** A slow-moving radial gradient that simulates a sunbeam passing over the garden.
* **Implementation:** A pseudo-element covering the background with `background: radial-gradient(circle at var(--mouse-x) var(--mouse-y), rgba(212, 175, 55, 0.15), transparent 40%)`.
* **Interaction Behavior:** On desktop, the focal point of the light softly tracks the user's cursor with a high delay (`ease: "power2.out", duration: 1.5` in GSAP). On mobile, it slowly orbits the center of the viewport based on the device's gyroscope (DeviceOrientation API), creating physical depth.

### 3.2 Floating Pollen & Dust Particles
* **Visual Behavior:** Tiny, warm specks of light drifting upward and sideways.
* **Motion Behavior:** Particles must follow a sine-wave path, not a straight linear line.
* **Implementation:** Use a Three.js particle system or HTML5 Canvas. Generating DOM nodes for particles will destroy mobile performance. Limit to 50 active particles on mobile, 150 on desktop.
* **Animation Timing:** Lifespan of 8000ms to 12000ms per particle. Fade in over 2000ms, drift, fade out over 2000ms.

### 3.3 Wind Simulation
* **Visual Behavior:** All interactive flowers and background foliage sway gently in unison.
* **Implementation:** A global CSS variable `--wind-angle` updated via requestAnimationFrame using a Simplex Noise algorithm.
* **Motion Behavior:** Transform origins must be set to the base of the stem (`transform-origin: bottom center`). The rotation varies between `-2deg` and `2deg`.

### 3.4 Butterfly & Firefly AI
* **Purpose:** To provide moments of serendipitous delight.
* **Butterflies (Day/Garden phase):** 2 or 3 small vector butterflies that follow bezier paths across the screen. They have a 5% chance every 10 seconds to land on an inactive UI element, resting their wings (scaleX oscillation) before flying away when the user hovers/taps near them.
* **Fireflies (Secret Ending phase):** Glowing orbs with a CSS `box-shadow` pulse that cluster around the birthday cake and final text.

## 4. The Flower & Bouquet System (Core Progression Mechanics)
Flowers are the primary UI mechanism. There are no traditional "Next" buttons; progression is tied to interacting with flowers.

### 4.1 The Bouquet State Machine
The application state revolves around the `BouquetState`.

* **Initial State:** An empty elegant glass vase sits in the lower corner of the UI, subtly blurred.
* **Interaction:** When the user completes a section (e.g., finishes reading the letter, listens to the voice note), a specific flower blooms on screen.
* **Transition:** Upon clicking/tapping the bloomed flower, it detaches, shrinks slightly, and animates via a GSAP `MotionPathPlugin` arc into the vase.
* **Culmination:** The final scene cannot be accessed until all 6 flowers are in the vase.

### 4.2 The Flower Library Specification
* **The Rose (Deep Love):** Appears after the Letter. Bloom animation: elastic ease (2400ms).
* **The Tulip (Perfect Affection):** Appears after the Scrapbook. Bloom animation: power3.inOut (1800ms).
* **The Peony (Prosperity):** Appears after the Memory Garden. Bloom animation: staggered SVG layers (3000ms).
* **The Baby's Breath (Everlasting Connection):** Accompanies the Distance map. Bloom animation: twinkling sequence.
* **The Daisy (Joy):** Appears after Music Player. Bloom animation: pop with spin (1200ms).
* **The Lavender (Calm):** Appears after Voice Note. Bloom animation: linear growth.

## 5. Scene-by-Scene Blueprint & Choreography

### 5.1 Scene 1: The Unboxing (Initialization)
* **Goal:** Curiosity/Anticipation.
* **Interaction:** Drag ribbon to untie, hold wax seal for 1500ms to crack.
* **Motion:** Box lid lifts, releasing warm light.

### 5.2 Scene 2: The Handwritten Letter
* **Goal:** Intimacy.
* **Interaction:** Scroll/swipe to reveal text word-by-word (ink-bleed effect).
* **Typography:** Dancing Script.

### 5.3 Scene 3: The Scrapbook & Memory Garden
* **Goal:** Nostalgia.
* **Interaction:** Draggable canvas (Desktop), swipe gallery (Mobile). Flip polaroids to see notes.

### 5.4 Scene 4: Across the Distance (Singapore → Jombang)
* **Goal:** Proof of effort.
* **Interaction:** Trace a golden thread between Singapore and Jombang icons.
* **Audio:** Heartbeat-like rhythmic pulse.

### 5.5 Scene 5: The Voice Note & Music Player
* **Goal:** Sensory connection.
* **Interaction:** Play button with waveform visualizer. Background music ducks (10% volume) while note plays.

### 5.6 Scene 6: The Final Bouquet & Birthday Cake (Secret Ending)
* **Goal:** Emotional peak.
* **Interaction:** Blow into mic or tap screen to blow out candles. Explosion of golden particles illuminates the final birthday wish.

## 6. Technical Engineering Architecture

### 6.1 Architecture
* **SPA (Single Page Application):** Essential for continuous audio.
* **DOM vs Canvas:** Canvas/WebGL for all environment/particles. DOM for all text, UI, and scrapbook elements.
* **State Management:** Singleton `GardenState.js` for progress tracking.

### 6.2 CSS & Animation
* **BEM Methodology:** Strictly enforced.
* **Performance:** Use `will-change: transform, opacity;` only during active animation. Layout thrashing is forbidden (animate only `transform` and `opacity`).
* **GSAP Timelines:** Mandatory for synchronization.

### 6.3 Audio
* **Initialization:** Must be triggered by the user (Unboxing).
* **Ducking:** Automatic volume ducking during voice notes.
